About this Website

It can be seen from the graph shown above that there is a positive linear relationship between the height of the ball from the sand and the average depth of the crater. This means that as the height of the ball from the surface (sand surface) increases, the average depth of the crater also increases. The r squared value shown in the graph above is 0.93 (rounded to two decimals points).

As the r squared value is close to 1, this suggests that there is a strong positive linear relationship between the height of the ball from the surface and the average depth of the crater.

The first strength of the methodology was related to how the ball was positioned before it was released onto the sand. The ball was positioned on the meter stick used to measure the length by positioning the bottom of the ball to just touch the mark for the height. For example for 20 cm from the surface, the ball was placed with its bottom exactly touching the 20 cm mark. Due to the fact that the way in which the ball was placed was controlled, the distance that the ball drops for each trial in the experiment (for specific variation) is the exact same. This creates a positive impact on validity because it makes eliminates the possibility of the distance of the ball dropping being wrong due to inconsistent placement, which could have possibly impacted the data derived from the experiment.
